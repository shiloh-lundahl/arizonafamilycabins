Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our cabins, booking process, and the White Mountains.

The Cabins

What cabins do you have?

We have two large-group cabin rentals in Lakeside, Arizona. Parkway Lodge has 7 bedrooms and sleeps up to 27 guests. Mohave Cabin with Treehouse has 7 bedrooms plus a treehouse and sleeps up to 33 guests. Both are located just west of Rainbow Lake.

What is the treehouse at Mohave Cabin?

The treehouse is a genuine sleeping structure set among the pines that adds extra capacity beyond Mohave's 7 main bedrooms, bringing total occupancy to 33. It's a standout feature — especially popular with kids and teens — and makes Mohave Cabin uniquely suited to large family reunions.

Can we book both cabins at the same time?

Yes. Combining Parkway Lodge and Mohave Cabin with Treehouse accommodates groups of up to 60 guests across two adjacent properties. Call us to discuss combined availability.

What amenities are included?

Both cabins include full kitchens, multiple bathrooms, living and dining areas, outdoor space, WiFi, and ample parking. Call us for the full amenity list for each cabin — details vary between the two properties.

Location & Getting There

Where exactly are the cabins?

Both cabins are located just west of Rainbow Lake in Lakeside, Arizona — in the Pinetop-Lakeside community in the White Mountains. Lakeside sits at approximately 7,000 feet elevation in the Apache-Sitgreaves National Forest.

How far is it from Phoenix?

Approximately 3.5 hours via US-60 East through Globe, then AZ-260 east to Show Low and Lakeside. It's a straightforward drive — no steep mountain passes or difficult road conditions in summer.

How far is it from Tucson?

Approximately 3 hours via AZ-77 North through Oracle and Globe, then east to Show Low and Lakeside.

Is Rainbow Lake walkable from the cabins?

Yes — both cabins are located just west of Rainbow Lake. It's a short walk to the water. The lake is 60 acres, stocked with trout, and great for fishing, kayaking, and enjoying the scenery.

What is there to do in the area?

Fishing and kayaking on Rainbow Lake and nearby lakes (Fool Hollow, Hawley Lake), hiking and mountain biking in the Apache-Sitgreaves National Forest, the Mogollon Rim overlooks, ATV trails, wildlife watching, and exploring the towns of Pinetop-Lakeside and Show Low.
